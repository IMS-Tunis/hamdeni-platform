3.2 Input and Output Devices

Computer Science IGCSE notes, prepared by Dr. Hamdeni

3.2a: Input Devices

  • An input device is a piece of hardware that allows data to be entered into a computer system. This means the device sends raw data to the computer so the CPU can work with it.
    • The data entered may be in different forms, such as text, images, sound, or movement. For example, moving a mouse sends movement data to the computer.
    • Input devices are essential because they send information into the computer for processing.
    • Some input devices are used by humans (e.g., a keyboard). Others, such as sensors, work automatically in real-time environments. Real-time means the device sends data continuously as events happen, without waiting.
  • A barcode scanner is used to read barcodes made of dark and light parallel lines that represent digits using specific patterns.
    • Each digit has a unique pattern of thick and thin lines. Digits on the left and right of the barcode use different codes to allow scanning in either direction. This allows the barcode to be read even if it is upside down.
    • When scanned, the barcode reflects red light differently between dark and light areas, which is captured by sensors and converted into digital data. The scanner detects differences in reflected light and converts them into 0s and 1s.
    • The barcode is looked up in a database where its record includes item name, price, quantity, and reorder level.
    • Barcode systems update stock levels and trigger automatic reordering when stock is low.
  • A QR code (Quick Response code) is a square-shaped matrix of black and white blocks that can hold much more data than a traditional barcode.
    • A QR code can store up to 4296 characters or 7089 digits, including web addresses or phone numbers. This is enough to store a long web address or contact information.
    • It includes three large squares in the corners to help the scanner detect the code's position and angle.
    • QR codes are often scanned using smartphone or tablet cameras, and decoded by QR applications into readable information.
    • QR codes use built-in error-checking so they are more reliable than traditional barcodes even if damaged.
  • A digital camera captures light using a lens and a light-sensitive array of pixels called a CCD.
    • Each pixel converts light into an electric signal. This signal is sent to an analogue-to-digital converter (ADC) to form a digital image. The CCD captures light, and the ADC converts the captured signal into digital form.
    • Digital cameras also measure colour using 24-bit RGB: 8 bits each for red, green, and blue levels. Each colour can have 256 different intensity levels.
    • More pixels usually mean higher image resolution and a larger file size. Image quality also depends on the lens and sensor.
    • Images can be transferred to computers using USB or Bluetooth and stored in formats like JPEG or RAW.
  • A keyboard is the most common method of entering text into a computer or device.
    • Pressing a key completes a circuit. The CPU detects the signal and matches it to a character using its ASCII value. For example, the letter 'A' is stored as the number 65 in ASCII.
    • Keyboards can be wired or wireless; virtual keyboards exist on phones and tablets using touch screens.
    • Repetitive keyboard use may cause injuries such as RSI (repetitive strain injury). Ergonomic keyboards can help reduce strain.
  • A microphone captures sound by detecting air vibrations and converting them into an electric signal.
    • Inside the microphone, a vibrating diaphragm causes a coil to move in a magnetic field, generating a current. In simple terms, when the diaphragm moves, it creates a small electric signal.
    • This analogue signal is then converted to digital using an ADC before the computer can store or process it.
    • Microphones can be built into the device or connected externally via USB or Bluetooth.
  • An optical mouse detects movement by capturing many images per second of the surface beneath it.
    • A red LED reflects light from the surface; a CMOS sensor reads the pattern and a processor calculates movement. By comparing images quickly, the mouse calculates how far it has moved.
    • Optical mice are more reliable than mechanical ones and can be used on most flat surfaces.
    • Wireless optical mice use Bluetooth, but wired ones avoid signal loss and do not need batteries.
  • Scanners convert physical documents or 3D objects into digital images for storage or processing.
    • 2D scanners use a CCD and light to scan flat pages, producing JPEG or editable text files using OCR. OCR converts a picture of text into actual editable characters.
    • 3D scanners scan solid objects across x, y, and z dimensions, producing 3D models for CAD design or 3D printing.
    • 2D scanners are often used in airports to match passport photos with live images using face recognition software.
    • In 2D scanning, a light shines on the document. Mirrors and lenses reflect the image onto sensors, which convert it into digital format.
  • A touch screen is an input device that detects user interaction through direct touch on the display surface.
    • Capacitive screens use a conductive layer that detects changes in electric fields caused by fingers or special styluses.
    • In a capacitive touchscreen, the screen stores electrical charge and detects coordinates when a finger touches the screen and completes the circuit at that point. Your finger changes the electric field at the touch point.
    • Infrared screens detect when a beam of light is interrupted by touch.
    • Resistive screens consist of two layers that create a circuit when pressed together.
    • Touch screens are used in mobile phones, tablets, ATMs, and kiosks to allow direct interaction without a keyboard or mouse.
  • Summary: Input devices send data into the computer system.

3.2b: Output Devices

  • An output device is hardware that shows or plays the results of the computer’s processing. Output devices present results but do not store data permanently.
    • Output can be in the form of text, images, sound, or physical actions.
    • These devices allow the system to communicate with the user or another system.
    • Some output devices create visible displays (like screens), while others perform physical tasks (like actuators).
  • An actuator is a mechanical component that performs an action, such as moving a part, opening or closing something, or switching something on or off. An actuator is considered an output device because it performs physical actions based on computer instructions.
    • Actuators are often used in systems that need physical changes, like controlling valves or motors.
    • A solenoid is one type of actuator that creates motion using magnetic fields.
    • Solenoids can produce linear or rotary motion depending on their construction.
    • Actuators receive signals from the microprocessor and respond by carrying out actions such as turning on a motor, opening a valve, or switching on lights.
  • Projectors are used to display digital output on large screens and come in two main types: DLP and LCD.
    • DLP projectors use a chip with thousands of tiny mirrors (DMD) that reflect light to form an image.
    • DLP mirrors tilt toward or away from light to produce light or dark pixels and switch rapidly to create greyscale and colour images. They can tilt thousands of times per second.
    • LCD projectors use coloured mirrors and separate LCD panels for red, green, and blue. These combine to form a full-colour image.
    • DLP projectors are known for high contrast and reliability; LCD projectors give better colour detail and energy efficiency.
  • Inkjet printers spray tiny droplets of liquid ink onto paper using either thermal bubble or piezoelectric methods.
    • A thermal bubble printer heats the ink to form bubbles that force ink onto the page.
    • A piezoelectric printer uses vibrating crystals to eject ink droplets.
    • Inkjet printers are ideal for printing photos or small documents in colour.
    • The print head in an inkjet printer moves back and forth across the paper, precisely controlling where each droplet of ink lands to form the image or text.
  • Laser printers use dry toner powder and static electricity to produce fast and high-quality printed pages.
    • A rotating drum is charged and then scanned by a laser, which removes charge in the shape of the image.
    • Toner powder sticks to the charged areas, then is transferred to paper using opposite charges. Opposite electrical charges attract each other.
    • A heated roller called a fuser melts the toner to make the print permanent.
    • Laser printers are typically used in offices due to their high printing speed, cost-efficiency over time, and ability to handle large print volumes.
  • A 3D printer creates solid objects by adding material layer by layer, based on a digital design.
    • The process starts with a CAD model that is converted into a format the printer can use. This is usually converted into a file such as STL.
    • Direct 3D printing uses inkjet-like movement to build the object vertically.
    • Binder 3D printing sprays powder, then a glue layer to solidify it.
    • 3D printers use materials like resin, metal powder, or plastic, and may require post-processing such as curing or cleaning.
  • Display screens can be LED, LCD, or OLED depending on how they produce images.
    • LED screens use red, green, and blue light-emitting diodes. Brightness is controlled by the current, allowing vivid colours.
    • LCD screens use liquid crystals and need backlighting, usually with LEDs, to display the image.
    • OLED screens use organic compounds that emit light when powered and do not need backlighting.
    • OLED screens are flexible, thin, and often use less power, which makes them suitable for smart devices and modern TVs. Because pixels produce their own light, they can turn off completely when displaying black.
  • A loudspeaker is an output device that produces sound by converting electric signals into air vibrations.
    • A DAC turns digital audio files into analogue electrical signals. Speakers can only work with analogue electrical signals.
    • These signals are amplified to a strong current and sent to a speaker cone.
    • The vibrating cone moves air to create sound waves that humans can hear.
    • Sound output may also be delivered through headphones, which use similar principles as loudspeakers but are designed for personal listening.
  • Summary: Output devices present the results of processing.

3.2c: Sensors

  • A sensor is a special input device that detects physical changes in the environment and sends that information to a computer system.
    • Sensors detect changes such as temperature, light, pressure, or movement and send continuous signals, not only when a change happens. They constantly measure the environment, even when nothing changes.
    • The data captured by sensors is analogue, meaning it can take many values, and it needs to be converted to digital using an ADC. For example, temperature can be 21.3°C, 21.4°C, and so on.
    • In control systems, feedback is used: the output affects the next sensor reading, creating a loop that helps adjust the system automatically. For example, heating turns off when the temperature becomes correct.
    • Sensors do not make decisions; they simply send data to a microprocessor which then determines the appropriate action.
  • Sensors are used in both monitoring systems and control systems.
    • Monitoring systems report when something is out of range, like a hospital monitor alerting a nurse. Monitoring systems inform humans; control systems act automatically.
    • Control systems adjust devices to bring the value back to the desired level, like an air conditioner turning on when it's too hot.
    • Monitoring systems can alert users when abnormal values are detected, while control systems use sensor data to automatically adjust the output to maintain desired conditions.
  • Different types of sensors are used for specific applications:
  • Temperature sensors measure heat levels.
    • Used in greenhouses, heating systems, and industrial processes.
    • Can also be used in medical applications like digital thermometers or body temperature monitoring systems.
  • Moisture and humidity sensors detect water levels in air or soil.
    • Used in agriculture, food factories, and microchip manufacturing.
  • Light sensors measure brightness and trigger actions.
    • Used in automatic streetlights or headlamps on cars.
    • Light sensors are also used in alarm systems or display brightness control.
  • Infrared sensors detect invisible IR radiation.
    • Active sensors detect broken beams; passive sensors detect heat. Active sensors emit IR light, while passive sensors only detect existing heat.
  • Pressure sensors measure force from solids, liquids, or gases.
    • Used in weighing systems and safety checks.
  • Acoustic sensors detect sound.
    • Used in security alarms or machinery fault detection.
  • Gas sensors detect oxygen or carbon dioxide levels.
    • Used in pollution monitoring or car emissions.
    • Gas sensors can also be used in safety systems to detect leaks in factories or households.
  • pH sensors measure how acidic or alkaline a substance is.
    • Used in soil testing and chemical plants.
  • Magnetic field sensors detect changes in magnetic strength.
    • Used in CD players, mobile phones, and braking systems. For example, a phone can detect when a flip cover is closed.
  • Accelerometers detect motion or changes in velocity.
    • Used in airbag systems and phone screen rotation. They detect tilting, shaking, or sudden stops.
  • Proximity sensors detect nearby objects without touching them.
    • Used in mobile phones to turn off the screen when near the ear.
    • Proximity sensors are also used in parking systems to detect obstacles and alert drivers.
  • Flow sensors measure how much gas or liquid is moving.
    • Used in hospital devices and pipe flow systems. They measure the rate of flow, such as litres per minute.
  • Level sensors check liquid levels in tanks or containers.
    • Used in fuel tanks, medicine production, and leak detection.
  • Summary: Sensors detect environmental changes and send data to the system.
