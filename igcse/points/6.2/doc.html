6.2.a Understand what is meant by robotics

  • Robotics is a branch of computer science that focuses on the design, construction and operation of robots.
  • Robots can operate in various ways:
    • A robot can be independent, performing tasks on its own.
    • A robot can be dependent, requiring human control.
    • A robot may also be a combination of both.
    • A robot is described as automated when it can perform actions without human intervention.
  • Robots are used in many environments:
    • Factories
    • Homes
    • Farms
    • Hospitals
    • Entertainment
  • The term "robot" originates from the Czech word "robota", meaning "forced labour".
  • Asimov's Laws are fictional rules for robots:
    • A robot may not harm a human being.
    • A robot must obey orders given by humans unless it conflicts with the first law.
    • A robot must protect its own existence unless it conflicts with the first or second law.
  • Artificial intelligence (AI) is not the same as robotics. Most robots perform repetitive, preprogrammed tasks, not intelligent decisions.
  • A drone can either be controlled manually or operate using sensors and pre-programmed instructions. This shows how a robot may be partially or fully autonomous.
  • The word 'robot' was born in a play ‚Äî literally! Imagine naming your future project after a 100-year-old sci-fi script.
  • Think of a robot as your digital stunt double ‚Äî built to do the boring, dangerous, or downright exhausting things you‚Äôd rather avoid.

6.2.b Describe the characteristics of a robot

  • A robot typically includes the following key characteristics:
    • Mechanical structure: Provides form and movement (e.g., arms, wheels, legs).
    • Electrical components: Enable sensing and movement.
      • Sensors: Gather input from the environment.
      • Actuators: Perform actions such as turning wheels or arms.
      • Microprocessors: Process sensor input and issue commands.
    • Programmability: Robots follow stored instructions or code.
    • Most robots also have the ability to move, interact with surroundings, and repeat tasks.
  • A robotic arm with motors and proximity sensors moves boxes from one place to another based on programmed logic.

6.2.c Understand the roles that robots can perform and describe the advantages and disadvantages of their use

  • Robots are used in a wide range of areas:
    • Industry: Welding, assembling, packaging, testing, and transporting goods in warehouses.
    • Agriculture: Planting, weeding, harvesting, phenotyping, and drone-assisted crop monitoring.
    • Medicine: Assisting with surgery, diagnostics, sample collection, targeted therapy, and disinfection.
    • Domestic use: Cleaning floors, mowing lawns, ironing, and handling other chores.
    • Entertainment: Filming, lighting, stunts, animation, amusement park interaction, and educational kits.
    • Transport: Autonomous cars, trains, and airplanes using sensors and microprocessors.
  • A robot can use sensors and a microprocessor to control its actions:
    • A proximity sensor is used to detect nearby objects (e.g., fences).
    • The sensor sends digitised data continuously to the microprocessor.
    • The microprocessor compares the input with stored reference values.
    • If the values are within range, the robot continues its task.
    • If values are outside the range, the microprocessor sends a signal to an actuator to stop or turn the robot.
    • This process repeats continuously until the robot is turned off.
  • Advantages of using robots:
    • Robots can plant seeds more accurately than humans.
    • They can work continuously without breaks (24/7 operation).
    • They don‚Äôt get bored or distracted when doing repetitive tasks.
    • They can safely work in hazardous environments (e.g., near chemicals or animals).
    • Using robots can reduce labour costs since wages don't need to be paid.
    • They can enhance safety by taking over physically demanding or dangerous jobs.
    • Humans can focus on more skilled or creative tasks while robots do the repetitive ones.
  • [MARK-SCHEME-INFO] Robots may be expensive to purchase and set up.
  • Disadvantages of using robots:
    • They may require costly maintenance and expert repairs.
    • Robots may replace human jobs, leading to unemployment.
    • Workers may become deskilled if robots do most of the work.
    • If a robot malfunctions, tasks must be done manually, causing delays.
    • Robots may not be able to handle unexpected or non-standard tasks.
    • Changing a robot‚Äôs function may require additional time and expense.
    • A failure in one part of the automated system could stop the entire process.
  • In a robot factory, each robot may be responsible for a specific repetitive task, such as placing a biscuit into a box.

  • In medicine, a surgical robot allows a remote surgeon to perform an operation from another city.
