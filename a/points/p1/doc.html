1.1 Understand how and why computers use binary to represent all forms of data

  • Computers process and store all data in binary because internal hardware like logic gates and switches can only recognize ON/OFF states.
  • In binary, 0 represents OFF and 1 represents ON.
  • Any form of analogue data must be converted to digital data so computers can process it.
  • Logic gates control the flow of electricity and process digital data.
  • Registers are used to hold binary data during processing.
  • 💡 A switch ON = 1, OFF = 0.
  • Analogue data is like a dimmer light switch (smoothly adjustable), while digital data is like a standard switch (just ON or OFF).
  • Analogue data
  • Digital data

1.2 (a) Understand the denary, binary and hexadecimal number systems

  • Denary is used in everyday life, with each place increasing by powers of 10.
  • 🔢 The denary number 25177 = 2×10000 + 5×1000 + 1×100 + 7×10 + 7×1.
  • Binary is used by computers, with each place increasing by powers of 2.
  • Bit size
  • The MSB (Most Significant Bit) is the leftmost bit.
  • 💡 Binary 11101110 = 128+64+32+8+4+2 = 238 (denary).
  • Hexadecimal is closely related to binary; each hex digit is 4 bits.
  • 🔠 Hex digit A = 10, F = 15 (denary).
  • 🔄 1010 (binary) = A (hex) = 10 (denary).
  • Hexadecimal

1.2 (b) Convert between number systems

  • To convert binary to denary: add up the values of all positions where there is a 1.
  • ➡️ 011110001011 = 1024+512+256+128+8+2+1 = 1931 (denary).
  • To convert denary to binary: subtract the largest possible powers of 2, or divide by 2 and record remainders.
  • Successive division by 2 and reading remainders from bottom to top gives the binary value.
  • ➗ Denary 142: 142 ÷ 2 repeatedly gives remainders 1 0 0 0 1 1 1 0 (read bottom to top: 10001110).
